🌏 LET'S PLAN YOUR TRIP

Paro Tourism

Paro tourism offers everything in Bhutan, in terms of best tourist attractions & things to do, which a vacationer actually looks for while planning a holiday. This beautiful destination of Bhutan, Paro boasts more than 155 temples & monasteries, with some built as far back as the 14th century. The sight of Taktsang Monastery, or Tiger’s Nest, will surprise you as much as the national museum, Ta Dzong. Drugyel Dzong, or, The Fortress of the Victorious Bhutanese, is another attraction.

Things to Do on Holiday to Bhutan

Tharpaling Thongdrol Festival Bhutan

A one-day festival held in the Tharpaling Monastery of Bumthang, Thapaling Throngdoel festival is unlike other popular festivals of Bhutan. On this day the giant throngdrel or silk thangka of Jowo Jampa and Kunkhyen Longchen Rabjam are displayed. Tourists who visit Bumthang during this festival will get an insight into the rituals and ceremonies particular to Buddhism.

Punakha Tshechu Festival Bhutan

Held in Punakha Dzong, the festival marks the birth anniversary of Guru Rinpoche. Upon attending the Punakha Tshechu, one can witness the colourful and exciting Mask Dance and enjoy listening to the folk songs sung by women. The three-day festival let the tourists come close to to the rich culture and tradition of Bhutan.

Punakha Drubchen Festival Bhutan

Holding a historical significance, Punakha Drubchen Festival is celebrated to commemorate the victory of Bhutanese over the invading Tibetan armies. Upon attending the festival, tourists will not only learn about the rich culture of Bhutan but can also relive the era dating back to the 17th-century. The popular and colourful Mask or Cham dance and other folk dances are the key attraction of the festival that travellers would get to witness.

Best Time to Visit

Nestled in a valley, Paro enjoys salubrious weather throughout the year. September to November is an ideal time to visit Paro. The clear skies and balmy weather with temperature just under 20 degrees offer a perfect setting to go sightseeing or embark on a trekking expedition. Those seeking a cultural spectacle must plan a trip around March, when the popular annual fair, Paro Tshechu, is held.

How to Reach Paro Tourism?

Home to Bhutan’s only international airport and boasting good road connectivity, Paro can be reached with ease. Paro is the only town in Bhutan that has an airport. Druk Air (Royal Bhutan Airlines) is the main airline operating here, which offers daily flights to and from the major Indian cities like New Delhi, Kolkata, Guwahati and Bagdogra.
