FAQs for Sikkim Tour Packages

Q. When do Nathula Pass and Gurudongmar lake open for tourists in Sikkim?

+

Often the roads leading to Nathula Pass and Gurudongmar Lake close when it snows in the winter season making the two attractions inaccessible. Therefore, the best time to visit Nathula and Gurudongmar Lake is March to May and October to November.

Q. How to get a permit for Nathula Pass?

+

Special permits for domestic visitors have to be obtained from the Tourism Department and Police Check Post. International Tourists are not allowed beyond Tsomgo Lake.

Q. Are private vehicles allowed in Sikkim for sightseeing?

+

Private vehicles (only from Sikkim State) are allowed at the places that are not under protected areas in Sikkim. In protected areas like North Sikkim and some parts of East Sikkim, private vehicles are not allowed.

Q.What are the best religious places to visit in Sikkim?

+

The best religious places to visit in Sikkim are:

Ganesh Tok

Hanuman Tok

Solophok Chardham

Kirateshwar Mahadev Mandir

Rumtek Monastery

Pemayangtse Monastery

Q. When is the best time to enjoy snowfall in Sikkim?

+

The best time to enjoy snowfall in Sikkim is between October and March.

About Sikkim Tourism & Tour Packages

Sikkim is a picturesque state located in the northeastern part of India and is known for its natural beauty and cultural diversity. The state is known for its stunning landscapes, which include snow-capped mountains, lush green forests, and meandering rivers.

Sikkim is also home to several important Buddhist monasteries and is a pilgrimage destination for many travellers. From the nature to pilgrimage sites, Sikkim tourism spreads across the whole state, including a vast range of places.

Sikkim is also home to Mt Kanchenjunga, the world’s third highest mountain. The northeastern state of Sikkim offers opportunities to witness the rich culture of Lepchas, Bhutias, and Nepalese; exquisite Buddhist monasteries; sacred alpine lakes; and high mountain passes.

It unfolds unforgettable experiences of trekking, motorbiking, and mountain biking on its winding trails; savouring delectable cuisine; and a peek inside its rich traditional art and craft.
