FAQs For Uttarakhand Tour Packages

Q. How to get to Uttarakhand?

+

Uttarakhand is well-connected by airways, railways and roadways. Dehradun Airport connects Uttarakhand with some major cities in India. The Pantnagar Airport also connects the Kumaon part of the state to some of the cities in the country. There are taxis and buses available from the neighbouring states for Uttarakhand. Haridwar, Dehradun, Kathgodam, Haldwani along with Tanakpur and Rishikesh are the railway stations connecting the state to other parts of the country.

Q. What is the best time to plan a holiday to Uttarakhand?

+

Uttarakhand can be visited all throughout the year. However, the best time to plan a holiday to Uttarakhand is between October to June. In the monsoon months from June to September, Uttarakhand receives heavy rainfall which further leads to landslide and roadblocks. However, the rainy season is the time for Chota Chardham Yatra. It is also the time of opening of many treks. Therefore, it is recommended to visit Uttarakhand in the monsoon season also but practice caution while planning a trip.

Q. Are any special permits required to trekking in Uttarakhand?

+

Yes, for many treks in Uttarakhand, especially those in Uttarkashi district and along the border side, a permit or permission from the SDM office is required. However, it is to be noted that permit or permission for every trek is not required in Uttarakhand.

Q. When does Char Dham Yatra of Himalayas begin in Uttarakhand?

+

Akshaya Tritiya or Akha Teej marks the beginning of Char Dham Yatra in Uttarakhand, which usually commences in the months of April or May. The Chota Chardham that comprises Badrinath, Kedarnath, Yamunotri, and Gangotri opens between last week of April to October/November.

Q. When can one see snowfall in Uttarakhand?

+

Snowfall in Uttarakhand can be witnessed during the winter months of December, January, and February in destinations like Auli, Kausani, Khirsu, Chopta to Tungnath to Deoriatal, Chakrata to Kanasar, Kanatal to Dhanolti, Landour to Nag Tibba, Harsil to Dharali, Chaukori to Munsiyari, and more.

About Uttarakhand Tourism Packages

Fondly known as Dev Bhoomi, Uttarakhand is a land of various ancient shrines and other holy destinations. However, there is more to Uttarakhand than meets the eye. Besides providing pilgrimage opportunities through Chardham circuit, Haridwar and Rishikesh, this North Indian State greets the travelers with various splendid hill stations like Ranikhet, Almora, Nainital, Kausani, Mukteshwar, Binsar Mussoorie and Auli.The opportunities for adventure present itself in Uttarakhand in the form of white water rafting, trekking tours and skiing, besides other activities which give you the ultimate adrenaline rush and thrill.

Gorgeous views of snow capped peaks, meadows, valleys, rivers, and a rich colonial heritage in many of them, the hill stations of Uttarakhand win the hearts of both nature lovers and honeymooners. Jim Corbett National Park is one the best retreat for wildlife lovers in Uttarakhand. It is the oldest national park & first tiger reserve of India. Recently, it was in the limelight when the Prime Minister of India, Narendra Modi & Bear Grylls, host of the famous show, Man Vs Wildlife, appeared together in an episode shot in this tiger reserve in India.

Uttarakhand is also one of the best destinations when it comes to experiencing the tremendous benefits of Yoga and Ayurveda. Rishikesh in Uttarakhand is famously known as the Yoga Capital of the World, and is dotted with several ashrams & yoga centers where you can learn Yoga and meditation right from the beginning. The tranquillity and spirituality pervading the atmosphere make it a haven for those wanting to learn Yoga in the company of nature.
